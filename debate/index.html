Abstract

Everyone has the right to their opinion. Provide and defend your opinions. Debate it out in the way of the British parliament. Remember to base your opinions with facts and knowledge.

Rules and Regulations

  • Only one team from each institute can participate in this event.
  • Each team should consist of two members.
  • The format will be a version of the British Parliamentary debate. Two teams will argue against each other, with one forming the Government-arguing for the motion and the other, the Opposition-arguing against it.
  • There will be three rounds in total.
  • The Government will begin by giving its statement which will be followed by the Opposition’s statement. Following this, the Government and the Opposition’s replies, i.e, the second speaker will respond to the arguments raised by the other team. Finally, the Opposition will give its summary and the Government will conclude the debate with its summary.
  • The prelims and semi-finals will be for 5+5+2 minutes, whereas the finals will be for 7+7+3 minutes.
  • Preparation time for prelims and semi-finals will be 10 minutes, whereas for the finals, it will be 20 minutes.
  • Each member of a team is required to give at least one of the speeches.
  • No new points can be raised during the summary.
  • The first and last minute of each speech is “protected time” during which no Points of Information (POIs) may be offered to the speaker. POIs are also not allowed during the summary. During the rest of the time, the opposing team can interject the speaker with a question or an argument which the speaker may choose to accept. Replies to POIs should not go over 1 minute during which no cross questioning by the other team is allowed.
  • Barricading is not allowed. There should be at least 15 seconds between two consecutive POIs.
  • The judgment will be based on content, oratory flair, response to POIs, and overall presentation.
  • Participants are requested to gather knowledge about this debate version so that they can be fully prepared.
